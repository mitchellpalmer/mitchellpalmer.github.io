My New Zealand Election 2020 Analysis

In October 2020, New Zealand held a general election. Jacinda Ardern’s Labour Party was reelected as the major governing party and, for the first time since the implementation of New Zealand’s MMP electoral system in 1996, a major party held a majority of the seats in the House of Representatives. The main opposition party, the National Party, had a catastrophic result – losing 21 seats and 18% percentage points from their 2017 result.

I wrote two main pieces of analysis of this election. The (first)[https://rpubs.com/mitchellpalmer/677060] showed the geographic distribution of the results and analysed which candidates from the two major parties did well. The (second)[https://mitchellpalmer.shinyapps.io/Election2020Interactive/] matched the election results with data from the Census 2018 to offer some initial insights into the demographic makeup of each of the parties' coalitions.
